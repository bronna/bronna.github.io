In a recent talk by John Burn-Murdoch he presented a study that showed Republicans were 3-4x more likely to accept climate science when presented with a chart vs. a paragraph.

The power of data can cut multiple ways. In this article by Eugene Ludwig, he points out that economic experts claim to “deal with the economy by hard numbers.” This has led, he argues, to political leaders systematically overlooking the lived economic hardships many Americans have experienced for the past several decades.

Charts give off the illusion of objectivity. That’s not to say they’re not incredibly useful, just to acknowledge that every chart created is an interpretive act framing a mere slice of reality, and that can have powerful implications.

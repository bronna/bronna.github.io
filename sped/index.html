Data for Inclusion in Special Education

  • D3.js
  • React
  • Javascript
  • Data Analysis
  • UX/UI
  • Python
  • API's
  • QGIS

Soon after my youngest child was born, he was diagnosed with several disabilities. Meeting up with other parents of of children with similar disabilities, I started to grasp what my child's future would look like. Something that came up over and over was the inclusivity of their education. It bewildered me to hear of how different these children’s experiences were. Some were bussed off to a school in the district with all of the other disabled kids, isolated from their communities. Some went to school with their non-disabled sibling and had an aid in a mainstream classroom. Some had been moved from school to school as their parents had struggled to find a program in which they would progress. Some pulled their child out of school altogether, feeling that the options available were traumatizing their child more than helping.

Wondering if there was data to shed light on the vast differences between districts, I started to search. I found that the federal government mandates each district to report "special education" metrics, including rates of inclusion and graduation rates. Some states make this data easy to access, some do not. Knowing that data is a great tool for advocacy, a tool that many families haven’t had access to, I decided to start making an easy way to access it.

During the Lede Program I learned how to access APIs, scrape websites, automatically download and scrape documents, attach data to geographies, and then clean the data and put it in easier-to-access formats. Afterwards, I built out my frontend skills more, using D3 and React to visualize the data and make it interactive.

Now, I’m processing data from more US states. In addition to being personally meaningful, this project has been a fulfilling way to integrate so many technologies (geodata! pandas! ux!) into a coherent application.
